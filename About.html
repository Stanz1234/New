About Paoay


Paoay, Ilocos Norte: A Journey Through History and Culture


Located in the province of Ilocos Norte, the town of Paoay is a captivating destination that unfolds a rich tapestry of history, culture, and natural wonders. As one explores its narrow streets and vibrant neighborhoods, the unique blend of Spanish colonial influences and local charm becomes evident.


At the heart of Paoay's cultural heritage lies the iconic St. Augustine Church, affectionately known as Paoay Church. Built in 1694 by the Augustinian friars, this architectural marvel showcases a distinctive Baroque style, featuring massive buttresses strategically designed to withstand earthquakes. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the church stands as a symbol of devotion and craftsmanship that has endured for centuries.

Adjacent to the town, the Culili Point Sand Dunes stretch along the coastline, offering a dynamic contrast to the ancient Paoay Church. Beyond their scenic appeal, these sand dunes provide an adventurous playground for activities such as sandboarding and 4x4 rides, adding a modern thrill to the town's historical backdrop.

Paoay Lake, locally known as Dacquel a Danum, adds a touch of serenity to the landscape. Surrounded by lush greenery, the freshwater lake invites visitors to enjoy boating and immerse themselves in the tranquility of the natural surroundings. It serves as a peaceful retreat, complementing the vibrant energy of the town.

Once the official residence of former President Ferdinand Marcos, this grand structure now serves as a museum, offering a glimpse into the Marcos era through displays of memorabilia and artifacts.

Wandering through Paoay's streets, one encounters a collection of heritage houses that reflect the town's colonial past. These ancestral homes, with their timeless architecture and design, provide a glimpse into a bygone era and contribute to the town's cultural mosaic.

The annual Paoay Fiesta, dedicated to St. Augustine, adds a festive touch to the town's cultural calendar. With religious processions, cultural events, and lively festivities, the community comes together to celebrate its shared history and traditions.

In essence, Paoay, Ilocos Norte, invites visitors on a journey through time, where the past and present coexist harmoniously. From the awe-inspiring Paoay Church to the thrilling sand dunes and the tranquil Paoay Lake, the town offers a diverse range of experiences. Paoay stands not only as a destination but as a living testament to the resilience of Philippine heritage, where history, culture, and natural beauty converge to create a truly memorable experience for those who venture into its embrace.
